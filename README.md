1. A key pattern in my inspiration is the balance between real life and digital life. Many of my references, such as screen-based environments, reflect how people divide their time between physical needs and online activities. This leads me to a game concept where the player must manage both real-life responsibilities—such as eating and sleeping—and online engagement, like watching streams or interacting in virtual spaces. In terms of gameplay, this suggests resource and time management mechanics, where players must decide how to allocate their attention between these two worlds. I am interested in contrasting realistic environments with more stylized digital spaces, highlighting the tension between maintaining real-life well-being and staying connected online.
2. My classmate is interested in creating a strategy-based ghost hunting game inspired by Supernatural, where players manage a team and plan routes and actions to capture ghosts. While our ideas are different in genre, we share a similar interest in systems-based gameplay and player decision-making. Their concept focuses on strategic planning and coordinating actions, while mine focuses on balancing real-life and digital activities. Both ideas involve managing multiple elements at once and making meaningful choices that affect outcomes. 
3. The LA mentioned that he enjoy playing cooperative FPS games like Deep Rock Galactic and GTFO, which focus on teamwork and coordination. While my own interests lean more toward digital identity and balancing real-life and online experiences, we share a common interest in interactive systems and player engagement. Both of our interests involve players actively making decisions and responding to dynamic situations, whether through coordinating with teammates in a mission or managing different aspects of life and digital spaces.
